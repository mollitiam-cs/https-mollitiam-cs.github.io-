Operational Resilience

Within the UK, there is more regulatory focus and scrutiny on Operational Resilience than ever before. Financial institutions are now required to do more in this space, and clear guidelines and expectations have been set by the FCA and PRA, which now need to be met.

Those firms in scope of these regulations, need to complete their initial assessments by March 2022, and should now be working towards the March 2025 deadline. This will take time as you navigate various complexities and some unique challenges, however this should also be seen as a real opportunity for simplification and standardisation, especially with those processes which are treated a BAU, as they are not often reviewed. Therefore, this is an opportunity to pause and reflect, and drive improvements, as well as compliance with the new requirements.

If you are looking for some SME guidance or support for a specific project, or programme of activities you intend to complete by March 2025, please do reach out. We are more than happy to talk through your requirements and build out bespoke options for you, to ensure value add, as well as regulatory compliance.

Although the UK regulation is very much focused on the financial services sector, the principles are sound and can be considered best practice, and something which could be applied across any sector. If you have appetite to apply these types standards to your business, then please do get in touch.

Operational Risk

Supporting you

Operational Risk runs through everything you do in your business and can occur within any process or activity. It is therefore critical that operational risks are quickly identified, assessed, managed and reported. Having worked in operational risk for many years, we can help you identify where enhancements to your processes can be made following an event or breach, or support you in creating a more robust operational risk framework and control environment.

Although there is a significant focus on operational risk within the banking and finance sector, these types of risks can occur in any sector, in any business, as they are driven by inadequate process, failed controls or systems, and can originate from both internal or external events.

Identifying risk

We have extensive experience in providing operational risk training to embed a better understanding of why these risks need to be reported. We can help you identify and understand volume and value of such risk events, trends, potentially unidentified hot spots, as well as creating a no blame culture to encourage more reporting. If these types of risk go unreported, then it's impossible to fix them, improve the risk profile / landscape, and prevent reoccurrence.
